Question 5: Solving logarithmic equations.

[M1] Use of change of base rule or power rule: \( \log_2(x^2) = 2\log_2(x) \)
[M1] Setting up the quadratic equation in terms of \( \log_2(x) \). Let \( y = \log_2(x) \).
\( y^2 - 3y + 2 = 0 \)
[A1] Factorizing: \( (y - 1)(y - 2) = 0 \implies y = 1 \text{ or } y = 2 \)
[A1] Solving for x: \( x = 2^1 = 2 \) or \( x = 2^2 = 4 \)
