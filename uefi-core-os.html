Questions — A.1 Kinematics SL/HL (Paper 1) IB DP

Question 1: A block of mass $2.0\text{ kg}$ accelerates uniformly at a rate of $1.0\text{ m/s}^2$ when a force of $4.0\text{ N}$ acts on it. The force is doubled while resistive forces stay the same. What is the block's new acceleration?

Correct Answer: B ($3.0\text{ m/s}^2$)

Explanation: Initial net force $F_{\text{net}} = m \cdot a = 2.0 \times 1.0 = 2.0\text{ N}$. Since Applied Force $= 4.0\text{ N}$, Resistive Force $f_r = 4.0 - 2.0 = 2.0\text{ N}$. When applied force doubles to $8.0\text{ N}$, $F_{\text{net, new}} = 8.0 - 2.0 = 6.0\text{ N}$. Thus $a_{\text{new}} = \frac{6.0}{2.0} = 3.0\text{ m/s}^2$.

Strand 2: Digital Technology & Electricity CBE Senior Sec

Question: In a Junior Secondary Science project in Kiambu County, students constructed a solar-powered circuit using a 12V battery. Explain how Ohm's Law applies when resistor load increases.

Competency Evaluation Criteria:

  • 1. Correct state of formula: $I = \frac{V}{R}$.
  • 2. Explanation that voltage remains constant while current drops inversely as resistance increases.
  • 3. Practical application to community energy conservation.
