Question 1 of 10

Topic: Kinematics SL | 1 Mark
A block of mass 2.0 kg accelerates uniformly at a rate of 1.0 m s⁻² when a force of 4.0 N acts on it. The force is doubled while resistive forces stay the same. What is the block's acceleration?

Detailed Solution

Correct Answer: B. 3.0 m s⁻²

Step-by-Step Explanation:

  1. First, determine the resistive force. Using Newton's Second Law: F_net = ma
  2. F_applied - F_resistive = ma
  3. 4.0 N - F_resistive = (2.0 kg)(1.0 m s⁻²)
  4. F_resistive = 4.0 - 2.0 = 2.0 N
  5. Now, double the applied force to 8.0 N.
  6. New F_net = 8.0 N - 2.0 N = 6.0 N
  7. New acceleration a = F_net / m = 6.0 N / 2.0 kg = 3.0 m s⁻²
