Unit 3: Quadratic Expressions & Equations (Strand ID: g10-quad)

Standard Practice Item Bank (10 Questions)
1. Factorize the quadratic trinomial expression completely: x2 - 5x - 6.
Find factors that multiply to -6 and sum to -5: (x - 6)(x + 1).
2. Find the roots of the equation by factoring methods: 2x2 + 5x - 3 = 0.
Split the middle term: 2x2 + 6x - x - 3 = 0 ⇒ (2x - 1)(x + 3) = 0. Roots: x = 12, x = -3.
3. Determine the specific constant term c that makes x2 + 8x + c a perfect square trinomial.
Take half the linear coefficient and square it: (8/2)2 = 16. Therefore, c = 16.
4. Solve the equation using the formal quadratic formula, leaving answer to 2 decimal places: x2 - 4x + 2 = 0.
Apply x = -b ± √b2 - 4ac2a: x = 4 ± √16 - 82 = 2 ± √2. Roots are 3.41 and 0.59.
5. Calculate the discriminant value for 3x2 - 2x + 5 = 0 and describe the nature of its roots.
Δ = b2 - 4ac = (-2)2 - 4(3)(5) = 4 - 60 = -56. Since Δ < 0, the roots are Complex/Non-real.
6. Factorize using the difference of two squares identity: 9x2 - 49.
Rewrite as squared terms: (3x)2 - (7)2 = (3x - 7)(3x + 7).
7. Solve by completing the square stage: x2 - 6x = 0.
Add 9 to both sides: (x - 3)2 = 9 ⇒ x - 3 = ±3. Roots: x = 0, x = 6.
8. If one root of the equation x2 + kx - 12 = 0 is 3, calculate the value of the missing parameter k.
Substitute 3 into equation: (3)2 + 3k - 12 = 0 ⇒ 9 + 3k - 12 = 0 ⇒ 3k = 3 ⇒ k = 1.
9. Convert the expression into vertex format by structuring perfect squares: x2 + 4x + 7.
Group terms: (x2 + 4x + 4) + 3 = (x + 2)2 + 3.
10. Find the non-zero root values for the simple system: 4x2 = 28x.
Rearrange: 4x2 - 28x = 0 ⇒ 4x(x - 7) = 0. Since it must be non-zero, x = 7.

Unit 4: Similarity and Enlargement (Strand ID: g10-sim)

Standard Practice Item Bank (10 Questions)
1. Two mathematically similar triangles have corresponding side lengths of 4 cm and 12 cm. Determine the scale factor k of enlargement.
k = 124 = 3. The scale factor is 3.
2. If the linear scale factor between two similar planes is k = 2.5, find the area ratio transformation constant.
Area ratio is defined by k2. (2.5)2 = 6.25.
3. Two similar cylinders have volumes of 50 cm³ and 400 cm³. Compute their linear scale factor ratio in lowest fractional terms.
Volume ratio k3 = 40050 = 8. Therefore, k = 3√8 = 2.
4. A model design blueprint has an area of 15 cm². If the actual area must scale up by a factor of k = 10, find the total scale area.
Actual Area = Blueprint Area × k2 = 15 × 102 = 1500 cm².
5. Triangle ABC is scaled down to XYZ. The area of ABC is 100 cm², and the area of XYZ is 36 cm². Find the length of side XY if AB is 15 cm.
Area ratio = 36100 = 0.36. Linear factor k = √0.36 = 0.6. Side XY = 15 × 0.6 = 9 cm.
6. A solid metal cone has a mass of 1.2 kg. A mathematically similar cone is made of identical density metal but has double the height. Determine its total mass.
Mass scales directly with volume, which uses k3. Height ratio k = 2, so mass multiplier is 23 = 8. New mass = 1.2 × 8 = 9.6 kg.
7. The surface areas of two spheres are in the ratio 4:9. Find the exact matching ratio of their respective volumes.
Given k2 = 4/9 ⇒ k = 2/3. Volume ratio k3 = (2/3)3 = 8:27.
8. If an enlargement transformation has center (0,0) and factor k = -2, locate the mapped coordinate of original point P(3, -1).
Multiply coordinates by scale factor: P' = (3 × -2, -1 × -2) = (-6, 2).
9. On a geographic map blueprint scaled at 1:20000, a path length measures 5 cm. Compute the actual terrain distance in kilometers.
Real length = 5 × 20000 = 100000 cm = 1000 m = 1 km.
10. A standard storage box holds a volume of 0.5 m³. A similar container is built with all linear dimenions increased by 40%. Find its structural volume capacity.
Linear multiplier k = 1.40. Volumetric scale multiplier = (1.4)3 = 2.744. Capacity = 0.5 × 2.744 = 1.372 m³.

Unit 5: Reflection and Congruence (Strand ID: g10-ref)

Standard Practice Item Bank (10 Questions)
1. Reflect the coordinate point A(4, -3) across the Cartesian standard line axis y = 0 (x-axis). State the new coordinate layout.
Reflection across x-axis negates y: (x, -y) ⇒ A'(4, 3).
2. Find the coordinate map of point B(2, 5) when processed through a reflection across the mirror line y = x.
Reflection across y = x swaps coordinate locations: (y, x) ⇒ B'(5, 2).
3. Point C(-1, 4) is reflected across the vertical mirror plane line x = 2. Track the path to locate the transformed position C'.
Distance from x=-1 to x=2 is 3 units. Move 3 units further past the line: 2 + 3 = 5. Map coordinate: C'(5, 4).
4. State the geometric congruence criteria being used if two triangles have all matching sides exactly equal.
The matching condition corresponds directly to the SSS (Side-Side-Side) congruence criteria theorem.
5. Triangle ABC has angles 40° and 60° with an included side of 8cm. Triangle PQR has angles 40° and 60° with an identical included side of 8cm. Name the congruence criterion.
Since the equal side is bounded explicitly between matching angles, it satisfies ASA (Angle-Side-Angle) criteria.
6. If point D(3, 2) transforms to D'(3, -6) via a linear reflection, define the explicit algebraic formula of the mirror line axis.
Find the midpoint of y-values: 2 + (-6)2 = -2. The mirror plane line is horizontal at y = -2.
7. Two right-angled triangles share a hypotenuse of length 13 cm and one corresponding leg of 5 cm. Identify the congruence condition.
This configuration satisfies the specialized RHS (Right-angle, Hypotenuse, Side) congruence axiom.
8. If triangle LMN is congruent to triangle XYZ, and the perimeter of LMN is 34cm, write the value of the structural perimeter of XYZ.
Congruent geometric figures maintain identical side values and geometric traits, so perimeter is exactly 34cm.
9. Reflect point E(-2, -5) across the coordinate horizontal mirror line y = -1.
The distance from y = -5 to y = -1 is 4 units upward. Move 4 units upward past line: -1 + 4 = 3. Position is E'(-2, 3).
10. True or False: All mathematically similar figures satisfy the structural geometric properties of congruent systems.
False. Congruent systems require absolute 1:1 scale equivalency, whereas similar systems can change scale size.

Unit 6: Rotation (Strand ID: g10-rot)

Standard Practice Item Bank (10 Questions)
1. Rotate point P(3, 5) by an angle of 90° clockwise around the origin center coordinates (0,0).
The mapping rule for 90° clockwise about origin is (x,y) ⇒ (y, -x). Thus, P'(5, -3).
2. Rotate point Q(-2, 6) through an angle of 180° around the coordinate center origin. Locate the target coordinate.
The transformation mapping rule for 180° rotation is (x,y) ⇒ (-x, -y). Thus, Q'(2, -6).
3. Transform point R(1, 4) by a 90° anticlockwise rotation around the tracking point (0,0).
The mapping rule for 90° anticlockwise about origin is (x,y) ⇒ (-y, x). Thus, R'(-4, 1).
4. A rotation maps point T(2, 3) to T'(-3, 2) around the origin. Determine the directional angle parameters of this transformation.
The coordinate shift follows (x,y) ⇒ (-y, x), which is a 90° anticlockwise rotation.
5. Rotate point A(4, 2) by 90° clockwise around a non-origin center fixed at coordinate pivot point (1,1).
Translate pivot to origin: (4-1, 2-1) = (3,1). Apply 90° clockwise rotation: (1, -3). Translate back: (1+1, -3+1) = (2, -2). Result is A'(2, -2).
6. State the coordinate mapping identity rule for a geometric rotation of 270° clockwise around center point (0,0).
Rotating 270° clockwise is identical to a 90° anticlockwise rotation: (x,y) ⇒ (-y, x).
7. Find the value of invariant coordinates when a shape rotates around its explicit center pivot point (3, -2).
The center of rotation itself is the only invariant location point. Coordinates are exactly (3, -2).
8. Rotate point M(-4, -1) by an angle of 180° around the center tracking node origin.
Negate both coordinate metrics: (-x, -y) ⇒ M'(4, 1).
9. If shape transformation matrix mapping brings node coordinates from (1,0) to (0,-1), state the equivalent rotational parameters.
The transformation shifts the unit vector down to the negative y axis, representing a 90° clockwise rotation.
10. Determine the coordinate location of point K(0, 3) rotated 90° anticlockwise around origin parameters.
Apply mapping rule (x,y) ⇒ (-y, x): K'(-3, 0).
